## 3. Risk, Threats, and Failure Modes

### 3.1. Critical Risks (Prioritized)

| Risk | Description | Severity | Mitigation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. "Agent App" is too complex for non-coders** | Hermes/OpenClaw may require Python and deployment knowledge. Non-technical buyers will churn. | **High** | Segregate offers: "Agent App Builder" (technical) vs. "Agent Automation" (no-code). Offer a "No-Code Hermes" path using n8n + OpenClaw UI. |
| **2. Platform dependency / open-source churn** | Hermes/OpenClaw may change APIs, break, or become abandoned. | **High** | Teach principles, not just tools. Focus on agent architecture (memory, tools, routing) that is tool-agnostic. Provide migration guides. |
| **3. "Solopreneur AI app" is a myth** | Most solo-built AI apps fail to get traction. Students build but don't sell. | **High** | Include a dedicated "Go-to-Market" module: Launch strategy, distribution channels, pricing experiments. Partner with a marketing expert. |
| **4. Market saturation (low-end)** | Courses like "AI Agent Academy" ($1,497) are already well-known. | **Medium** | Narrow to "apps," not agents. Position as "The only course that teaches you to build and sell agent apps." Charge premium for exclusivity. |
| **5. Technical debt & scaling nightmares** | Student apps work for 1 user but break at 10 users (rate limits, memory leaks). | **Medium** | Include a "Production Readiness" module: Caching, rate limiting, cost monitoring, error handling. Provide a "Scaling Checklist." |

### 3.2. Failure Modes to Avoid

- **The "Course Creator" Trap:** Don't just sell information. Sell a **system** and a **community**. Students must feel they are joining a movement, not just buying a video library.
- **The "Feature Creep" Trap:** Don't try to be everything to everyone. The core offer must be laser-focused on building and monetizing one type of product: the AI agent app.
- **The "Refund Race" Trap:** If the curriculum is too hard for non-coders, refund rates will spike. Ensure clear pre-requisites and a "No-Code Hermes" path.
