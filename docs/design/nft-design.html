03 · A·2 concept

    
Interference Field

    
Two sinusoidal wave systems derived from train_metric_value and holdout_metric_value. When they agree (low overfit), the waves align into clean color bands. When they diverge (high overfit), the phase difference creates vivid color warfare — the "war" between what the model learned in training and what it does in production. Win_rate tilts the base hue warm or cool.

    

      
wave A freq: f = 0.038 + train × 0.022

      
wave B freq: f = 0.038 + holdout × 0.022

      
phase shift: gap × π × 24

      
saturation: |wA − wB| × 0.82

04 · C·3 concept

    
Ghost Lineage

    
All strategies in an optimizer lineage rendered onto one canvas, drawn back-to-front. delta_sharpe normalizes to alpha — negative delta means near-invisible ghost, positive delta means solid presence. The dominant strategy (highest delta_sharpe) crowds out the ghosts visually. You see the evolutionary history as spectral residue beneath the winner. forward_return_agreement drives coherence bias; high-agreement strategies cluster toward center.

    

      
alpha: 0.08 + (delta − min) / range × 0.85

      
draw order: lowest delta first (back)

      
B: alpha ≈ 0.08 — ghost

      
A: alpha ≈ 0.46 — ancestor

      
C: alpha ≈ 0.92 — dominant

05 · B·2 concept

    
Knot Topology

    
action_validity measures how often the trader's decisions are mechanically valid signals. High validity → the trader and risk gate agree → unknot (simple circle, 0 crossings). Low validity → the system is fighting itself → complex torus knot (many crossings, winding number increases). Ribbon rendering: thick dark outer stroke, cream center highlight, z>0 segments drawn on top for visible over/under threading.

    

      
torus knot: x = (R + r·cos(qt))·cos(pt)

      
AV 0.94 → p=1 q=0 · unknot/circle

      
AV 0.91 → p=2 q=3 · trefoil

      
AV 0.71 → p=3 q=5 · complex
